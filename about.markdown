## Our Mission

**Vedic Wisdom for Families** is a family education platform dedicated to sharing authentic Vedic knowledge in modern English. Our goal is to make the profound wisdom of ancient India accessible, understandable, and applicable to contemporary family life.

---

- **Sacred Texts**: Vedas, Upanishads, Puranas, Bhagavad Gita
- **Philosophy**: Six Darshanas, Yoga Sutras, metaphysical concepts
- **Sciences**: Ayurveda, Jyotisha (astronomy/astrology), Mathematics, Vastu
- **Practices**: Rituals, festivals, mantras, meditation, yoga

- **Children**: Engaging stories and simple values

## The Vedic Tradition

The Vedic tradition represents one of humanity's oldest continuous wisdom traditions, with roots going back thousands of years. "Veda" means knowledge - specifically, knowledge of fundamental truths about existence, consciousness, ethics, and the path to fulfillment.

### Key Components

**The Four Vedas**
- Rigveda, Yajurveda, Samaveda, Atharvaveda
- Ancient hymns, rituals, and wisdom

**The Upanishads**
- Philosophical texts exploring reality and consciousness
- Over 108 Upanishads, with 13 considered principal

**The Epics**
- Ramayana: Story of Lord Rama's life and dharma
- Mahabharata: Including the Bhagavad Gita

**The Puranas**
- 18 Mahapuranas with stories, philosophy, and history
- Makes complex concepts accessible through narratives

**Philosophical Schools**
- Six Darshanas (perspectives) on reality
- Yoga, Vedanta, Samkhya, Nyaya, Vaisheshika, Mimamsa

**Applied Sciences**
- Ayurveda (health and medicine)
- Jyotisha (astronomy and timing)
- Yoga (union of body, mind, spirit)
- Vastu (architecture and space)

## A Note on Language

We present this wisdom in **English, not Sanskrit**. While Sanskrit terms are used when necessary (like "dharma" or "karma"), we always explain them clearly. Our goal is education, not language learning.

For those interested in Sanskrit, we provide:
- Proper transliteration
- Pronunciation guides
- Etymology when relevant
- Links to deeper study
